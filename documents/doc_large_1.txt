Section 1: Climate change is a long-term shift in global weather patterns. It is driven by a complex mix of natural variability and human activity. This report summarizes current observations, projections, and response options. Major causes include greenhouse gases from fossil fuel combustion, deforestation, and industrial processes. Carbon dioxide and methane trap heat and alter the energy balance of the planet. Observed effects include rising sea levels, increasing temperatures, more frequent heat waves, and shifts in seasonal rainfall patterns. Coastal communities face erosion and flooding risks. Ecosystems respond to warmer oceans and changing habitats. Mitigation strategies include renewable energy adoption, energy efficiency, electrified transport, and carbon capture. Policy and market incentives can accelerate these changes. This section reviews data sources and regional impacts for planning and risk management.
